# Instructor How‑To Manual (Frontline Boost / Project Tracking)

This manual walks you through the **instructor UI journey**: what you’ll see on-screen and what to click to review updates, leave feedback, and decide on purchase requests.

## What this app does (instructor view)

- **Student weekly updates**: students share what they did, what’s next, blockers, progress, and supporting links.
- **Review workflow**: you comment and set a clear outcome (pending / needs revision / approved).
- **Purchase requests**: you approve or reject requested items with an optional note.

## Access and accounts

### Sign in

1. Open the app.
2. Go to **Sign in**.
3. Sign in using the method your program uses (for example, email + password or a sign-in link).

After signing in, you’ll land on the **Instructor** dashboard.

### If you land on the wrong dashboard

If you sign in successfully but see a **Student** screen (or you’re missing instructor tabs), contact your program admin so they can enable instructor access for your account.

## Instructor dashboard overview

You’ll see three main tabs:

- **My projects**: projects in courses where you are assigned as an instructor.
- **Pending reviews**: weekly updates awaiting review.
- **My courses**: courses you are assigned to (and where you can manage students + projects).

Selecting a project opens a **project detail** view.

## Manage your courses (students + projects)

### Add students to a course

1. Open the **My courses** tab.
2. Find the course card you want.
3. In **Course students**, use **Add student to course…** to add a student.

You’ll see the student appear as a “pill” with their name and avatar.

### Remove students from a course

1. Open the **My courses** tab.
2. In the course card’s **Course students** section, click the **×** next to a student.

### Create a project (for your assigned courses)

1. Open the **My courses** tab.
2. Click **New project**.
3. Choose the **Course**, then enter **Project name** and **Description**.
4. Click **Create project**.

The project will appear under the course’s **Projects** list.

### Assign students to a project team

There are two convenient places to do this:

- **From My courses**
  1. Open **My courses**.
  2. Under **Projects**, click a project to open it.
  3. In the project page’s **Team** section, use **Add student to project…**.

- **From My projects**
  1. Open **My projects**.
  2. Click a project.
  3. In the **Team** section, use **Add student to project…**.

Tip: the “Add student” list is based on the **course roster**, so add students to the course first.

## Review weekly updates

### Open a project

1. From **My projects**, click a project card.
2. In the project view, scroll to **Weekly updates**.

Each update includes:

- **This week’s goals** (achieved / missed + reason)
- **Next week’s goals**
- **Blockers**
- **Overall project progress (%)**
- **Linked resources** (designs, docs, repos, demos, etc.)
- **Discussion** thread (comments)

### Leave feedback (comments)

1. Open the weekly update card.
2. Scroll to **Discussion**.
3. Type your feedback and click **Send**.

Good feedback usually includes:

- what was strong this week,
- what is unclear / missing evidence,
- next steps and expectations for next week.

### Set review status

At the bottom of the update, use **Set review status**:

- **Needs revision**: the team should fix and resubmit.
- **Pending**: keep as “to be reviewed” (useful if you commented but want revisions later).
- **Approve**: marks it approved and **locks** the decision.

Suggested workflow:

- Comment first, then set status.
- Use **Needs revision** when the update is incomplete (missing goals, missing evidence/links, unrealistic progress, etc.).

### Editing goals (instructor)

Instructors can click **Edit** on “This week’s goals” and adjust goals for clarity and correctness, then **Save changes**.

Use this sparingly—prefer comments + “Needs revision” so the team owns their report.

## Review purchase requests

Purchase requests are per-project and separate from weekly updates.

1. Open a project.
2. Scroll to **Purchase requests**.
3. For each request:
   - Optionally add a **note to team**.
   - Click **Approve** or **Reject**.

Important behavior:

- After you approve/reject, the request is **locked** (no more changes).

## Common “what you’re seeing” fixes (instructors)

### “I can sign in, but I see Student dashboard”

- Your account likely isn’t enabled for instructor access yet.
- Message your program admin and ask to be set up as an instructor and added to the right course(s).

### “I have no projects listed”

This usually means you haven’t been connected to any course yet.

- Ask your program admin to add you to the correct course(s). After that, projects should appear automatically.

### “Pending reviews is empty but students claim they submitted”

Verify:

- You’re looking at the correct project/course.
- The student selected the correct project before submitting.
- The update appears under the project’s **Weekly updates** list (even if it’s not in the “Pending reviews” tab).
